Testing Direction Links

The following demos showcase the app-direction-link component with various directions and configurations following the Queensland Government Design System.

1. Up Direction Link

A direction link pointing upward, commonly used for "Back to top" links. The arrow appears on the left side of the text.

Back to top 
<app-direction-link href="#top" direction="up">Back to top</app-direction-link>

2. Down Direction Link

A direction link pointing downward, used to guide users to scroll down or continue reading. The arrow appears on the left side of the text.

Read more 
<app-direction-link href="#content" direction="down">Read more</app-direction-link>

3. Left Direction Link

A direction link pointing leftward, used for "Back" navigation or returning to a previous section. The arrow appears on the left side of the text.

Back to previous 
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="left">Back to previous</app-direction-link>

4. Right Direction Link

A direction link pointing rightward, the default and most common variant. Used for "Continue", "Next step", or forward navigation.

Continue 
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Continue</app-direction-link>

5. All Directions Together

All four direction variants shown together, matching the Queensland Government Design System example. Useful for showing navigation through a process.

Up Down Left Right 
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="up">Up</app-direction-link>
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="down">Down</app-direction-link>
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="left">Left</app-direction-link>
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Right</app-direction-link>

6. Direction Links in a Sequential List

Direction links used within an unordered list to guide users through sequential steps or navigation items.

  • Step 1: Personal details
  • Step 2: Assessment
  • Step 3: Review
  • Step 4: Submit
<ul style="list-style: none; padding: 0;">
  <li><app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Step 1</app-direction-link></li>
  <li><app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Step 2</app-direction-link></li>
  <li><app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Step 3</app-direction-link></li>
  <li><app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Step 4</app-direction-link></li>
</ul>

7. Direction Link with aria-label (Accessibility)

When the visible text alone doesn't fully communicate the destination, an aria-label provides additional context for screen reader users.

Continue 
<app-direction-link href="/assessment/complete" direction="right"
  aria-label="Go to assessment completion page">Continue</app-direction-link>

8. Direction Link on Dark Background

Direction links automatically adapt their colors when placed on a dark background. The link text and arrow icon switch to light-colored tokens for proper contrast.

Continue to next section 
<div class="qld__body--dark" style="padding: 1.5rem;">
  <app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Continue to next section</app-direction-link>
</div>

9. Direction Link Opening in New Tab

Direction links can open in a new browser tab using the target attribute. This is appropriate for external resources or when you want to keep the current page open.

Visit qld.gov.au 
<app-direction-link href="https://www.qld.gov.au" direction="right" target="_blank">Visit qld.gov.au</app-direction-link>

10. Mixed Direction Links in Navigation Context

A realistic navigation pattern combining back (left), forward (right), and up direction links to show a breadcrumb-like flow through content.

Previous Home Next 
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="left">Previous</app-direction-link>
<app-direction-link href="#top" direction="up">Home</app-direction-link>
<app-direction-link href="#" direction="right">Next</app-direction-link>
