Testing Global Alert

The following demos showcase the app-global-alert component with various types, configurations, and usage patterns following the Queensland Government Design System.

1. Critical Alert with Action

A critical alert indicating an immediate threat to human life. Includes a dismissible close button and action link for more information.

<app-global-alert type="critical"
  message="This website is currently undergoing testing."
  actionLabel="Learn more"
  actionHref="#"></app-global-alert>

2. Warning Alert with Action

A warning alert for events that have happened or are about to happen, posing a threat to or seriously affecting a service.

<app-global-alert type="warning"
  message="Scheduled maintenance will occur on April 25, 2026 from 10pm to 2am."
  actionLabel="Learn more"
  actionHref="#"></app-global-alert>

3. General Information Alert with Action

A general information alert for low-level alerts, minor problems with the service, or important updates affecting content across an entire site.

<app-global-alert type="general"
  message="The KLPT assessment portal has been updated with new features."
  actionLabel="Learn more"
  actionHref="#"></app-global-alert>

4. Critical Alert Without Action

A critical alert without an action link, used when the urgent notice only requires awareness and no further interaction is needed.

<app-global-alert type="critical"
  message="Emergency: Severe weather warning in effect for your region."></app-global-alert>

5. Warning Alert Without Action

A warning alert without an action link, suitable for ongoing situations that users should be aware of but don't require immediate follow-up.

<app-global-alert type="warning"
  message="Service disruptions may occur during the upcoming holiday period."></app-global-alert>

6. General Information Without Action

A general information alert without an action link, ideal for persistent informational notices that don't require user interaction.

<app-global-alert type="general"
  message="New professional development resources are now available in the practice supports section."></app-global-alert>

7. Critical Alert with Rich HTML Content

A critical alert using projected content (ng-content) for rich HTML including bold text and links, matching the QLD Design System pattern.

Emergency: This website is currently undergoing critical testing. 
<app-global-alert type="critical"
  actionLabel="View details"
  actionHref="#">
  <strong>Emergency:</strong> This website is currently undergoing critical testing.
</app-global-alert>

8. Warning Alert - Non-dismissible

A warning alert that cannot be dismissed, used when the user must see the message (e.g., mandatory maintenance notice).

<app-global-alert type="warning"
  message="System maintenance scheduled for April 25, 2026."
  actionLabel="Learn more"
  actionHref="#"
  [dismissible]="false"></app-global-alert>

9. General Information - Non-dismissible

A general information alert that cannot be dismissed, suitable for persistent notices like cookie policy updates or terms of service changes.

<app-global-alert type="general"
  message="Our privacy policy has been updated. Please review the changes."
  [dismissible]="false"></app-global-alert>
