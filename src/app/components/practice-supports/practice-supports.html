Practice supports for reflective, intentional teaching

Intentional teaching happens when teachers and educators plan and act with purpose. It means thinking carefully about how each interaction, environment and learning experience supports children's learning, development and wellbeing. Teachers and educators use the KLPT to guide their decisions and plan the next steps in children's learning. They build on what they know about children's ideas, strengths and skills as well as the cultural and community knowledge children bring with them. This include recognising and valuing Aboriginal and Torres Strait ways of knowing, being and doing and working in partnership with families and communities. Intentional teaching strategies may include:

  • questioning
  • imagining
  • pausing
  • making connections
  • listening
  • collaborating

These strategies extend children's knowledge, skills and enjoyment of learning. They also reflect Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander pedagogies such as story sharing, using symbols and images, connecting to land and supporting non-verbal and visual learning. By responding to each child's strengths, interests and cultural background, teachers and educators ensure learning is meaningful and ongoing.

How to use these supports

The practice supports are intentionally broad. They are not written for one specific observable behaviour, but to offer options that educators can adapt using their professional judgement.

The strongest next steps usually come from combining observation evidence, knowledge of the child, and a clear understanding of the learning domain.
