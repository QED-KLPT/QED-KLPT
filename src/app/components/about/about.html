Kindergarten Learning Progression Toolkit: An overview

The KLPT is a Queensland Government initiative that provides teachers and educators with a voluntary, formative, child-centred assessment toolkit. It supports high-quality assessment practices and informs the planning cycle.

The KLPT has been developed in response to an identified need to enrich children's holistic learning development during the early years. The toolkit is designed to support teachers and educators in planning and practice by enhancing how they gather evidence and monitor what children 'know' and can 'do'.

Five core skills

The focus is on each child's individual learning journey. It is not designed to compare or benchmark children.

The toolkit monitors progress across five core skills:

  • Language and literacy
  • Executive function
  • Social and emotional learning
  • Physicality
  • Mathematics and numeracy

Alignment and supports

The KLPT is designed to complement and support existing practice. It gives teachers and educators a shared language for observation and assessment to support reflection and professional conversations.

The toolkit is aligned with:

  • Queensland kindergarten learning guideline
  • Early Years Learning Framework V2.0
  • The newly developed national tool

The KLPT includes foundational supports, fact sheets, informational videos, practical implementation supports, instructional videos and validated progressions that underpin an observation support tool.

Participant feedback

"The detailed feedback from the KLPT can provide valuable insights that inform the planning and implementation of the QKLG, helping educators tailor their teaching strategies to meet the needs of individual learners. Regular assessments with the KLPT can help monitor progress and make data-driven decisions, which is a key component of the QKLG's planning cycle."

The Kindy Learning Progression Tool is

  • a practical formative assessment tool
  • an assessment tool that focuses on core skills considered most significant for children's current and future learning success
  • a tool for monitoring an individual child's learning progress over time and celebrating their learning
  • a tool that supports the intentional planning of meaningful, inclusive and responsive learning experiences
  • a tool that recognises and builds on each child's strengths
  • a tool that celebrates growth over time in ways that honour identity, culture and community
  • a tool that supports opportunities to reflect on practice and pedagogy

The Kindy Learning Progression Tool is not

  • a test or summative assessment
  • a tool that replaces current approaches to observing and analysing children's learning, development and wellbeing
  • a tool to compare or rank children
